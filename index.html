TempEst-NEXT National Water Temperature Forecast

Note: forecasts are generally for smaller tributaries. If a forecast is at a confluence, the mainstem temperature likely differs. Note also that these forecasts are from a generic, national model. It is typically effective everywhere (e.g., mountains, deserts, forests, cities), with typical errors around 2-3 C (4-5 F), but any given site may have large errors. Forecasts just downstream of a reservoir, hot spring, or wastewater discharge may be particularly unreliable.

The below map is interactive and can be panned and zoomed. Hovering over a point will display its information in the top right; clicking will produce a persistent pop-up with the same information.

Forecast Summary

Stream Water Temperature = SWT. All metrics refer to daily or weekly average SWT at a site, so, for example, "national average maximum" is the national average of the maximum daily average SWT this week at each site.
